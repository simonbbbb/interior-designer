# Competitor Analysis: RoomSketcher (roomsketcher.com)

## Target Audience
Professionals (real estate agents, interior designers, contractors) primarily, homeowners secondary.

## Pros
- Most versatile input options in the market: AI Convert, Manual Trace, Professional Drawing Service, LIDAR Capture
- AI-powered floorplan conversion from uploaded images exists
- Works offline with sync (native iPad/Android apps)
- One-click measurement tools
- Professional-quality branded outputs with custom letterheads
- Short learning curve -- "no CAD experience required"
- Strong real estate industry partnerships (Keller Williams, RE/MAX endorsements)

## Cons / Weaknesses
- Pricing is expensive (pro-tier subscription model, not consumer-friendly)
- Free tier is extremely limited -- essentially a demo
- Wall editing is rigid -- modifying a converted floorplan requires going back to tracing step
- AI conversion quality inconsistent on complex/multi-story plans
- Browser-based editor less performant than Floorplanner's
- Furniture library is smaller and less diverse
- Geared toward "deliverables to clients" rather than "iterative home planning"

## Pricing Model
Freemium with heavy paywall. Professional subscription tiers. Designed for B2B.

## Floorplan Digitization
- AI Convert: Upload image, AI digitizes to editable plan
- Manual Trace: Upload and trace by hand
- Professional Service: Upload sketch, their team redraws it
- LIDAR Capture: Use device LIDAR to scan physical space
- Draw from scratch: Standard drag-and-drop editor

## Key Takeaway for HomeForge
RoomSketcher has the most complete input pipeline but is priced for professionals. Their AI conversion is a strong reference, but the post-conversion editing is clunky. We need better post-AI editing tools.
