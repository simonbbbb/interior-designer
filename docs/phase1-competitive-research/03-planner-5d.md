# Competitor Analysis: Planner 5D (planner5d.com)

## Target Audience
Consumer/homeowner first, prosumer second. Strong mobile-first positioning.

## Pros
- AI Floorplan Recognition -- upload image/photo and auto-convert to editable floorplan
- 8,000+ item interior decoration library
- Cross-platform: Web, iOS, Android, macOS, Windows, Apple Vision Pro
- 4K realistic rendering with advanced lighting/shadows
- Shopping lists with cost estimates (unique -- bridges planning to purchasing)
- High G2/Capterra ratings (4.4 stars) for ease of use
- 360-degree walkthroughs

## Cons / Weaknesses
- AI floorplan recognition struggles with non-standard architectural drawings
- Heavy browser load -- 3D rendering is resource-intensive, causes lag on mid-range machines
- Paywalled aggressively -- free version severely restricts furniture library and exports
- Wall editing after AI conversion is clunky -- walls often need complete redrawing
- No undo/redo history depth (limited to ~5 steps on free tier)
- AI recognition is "one-shot" -- if it fails, you start over
- Interface cluttered with upselling and premium prompts
- Desktop experience feels like a stretched mobile app, not native desktop

## Pricing Model
Freemium/Subscription-based. Free entry point but heavy paywall on pro features and full library access.

## Floorplan Digitization
AI Floorplan Recognition -- upload an existing image and the advanced AI tool automatically creates professional floor plans. One-shot conversion with no preview or adjustment before committing.

## Key Takeaway for HomeForge
Planner 5D proves AI floorplan recognition works technically, but their implementation is flawed: no preview, one-shot conversion, and poor post-conversion editing. Our AI pipeline must be transparent and adjustable.
